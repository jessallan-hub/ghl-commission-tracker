Scope, and where it stops

The flat $2,500 covers client success for the RT Digital active book, and that's it. That's deliberate: it keeps the number honest and keeps one fee from quietly turning into ten jobs. New ideas are genuinely welcome, and there are plenty of good ones, but anything outside CSM gets scoped and quoted on its own before I pick it up. More often than not I'll quote it at $0 and do it anyway. This isn't about charging for every extra; it's about keeping an active record of the work that sits above CSM. I'll go above and beyond gladly, but it should be recognised as exactly that: not as anything owing, but as context that counts when we next talk terms.

Covered by the flat $2,500 (CSM for the RT Digital active book):

  • Retention across the active book: check-in cadence, health reads, correct staging, saving at-risk clients.
  • Oversight of the RT Digital / Trade AI ads already running, making sure leads flow and land where they should.
  • The live revenue and commission dashboard.

Separate scope (welcome, but quoted and agreed before I start):

  • New offers or campaigns: the Google Ads product, outbound email and text sequences, landing pages, creative.
  • Build or dev work: websites, funnels, new CRM or workflow builds, product and onboarding setups.
  • Marketing or ad work for AT Network, or any entity other than the RT Digital active book.
  • Rolling out new products to partners (for example, white-label CRM).

How it works: you send the idea, I come back with scope and a price, we both say yes, then it starts. Nothing outside CSM comes out of the $2,500, and nothing starts without a yes on both sides.
